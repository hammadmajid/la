	x + y + 4z = 0

\[
	\begin{pmatrix}
		1  & -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} & 0 \\
		-1 & 2           & -3           & 0 \\
		1  & 1           & 4            & 0
	\end{pmatrix}
\]

$R_2 + R_1$:
\[
	\begin{pmatrix}
		1 & -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} & 0 \\
		0 & \frac{3}{2}  & -\frac{9}{2} & 0 \\
		1 & 1           & 4            & 0
	\end{pmatrix}
\]

$R_3 - R_1$:
\[
	\begin{pmatrix}
		1 & -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} & 0 \\
		0 & \frac{3}{2}  & -\frac{9}{2} & 0 \\
		0 & \frac{3}{2}  & \frac{11}{2} & 0
	\end{pmatrix}
\]

$R_2 \times \frac{2}{3}$:
\[
	\begin{pmatrix}
		1 & -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} & 0 \\
		0 & 1           & -3           & 0 \\
		0 & \frac{3}{2}  & \frac{11}{2} & 0
	\end{pmatrix}
\]

$R_3 - \frac{3}{2}R_2$:
\[
	\begin{pmatrix}
		1 & -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} & 0 \\
		0 & 1           & -3           & 0 \\
		0 & 0           & 10           & 0
	\end{pmatrix}
\]

$R_3 \times \frac{1}{10}$:
\[
	\begin{pmatrix}
		1 & -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} & 0 \\
		0 & 1           & -3           & 0 \\
		0 & 0           & 1            & 0
	\end{pmatrix}
\]

Back substitution:
\[
	z = 0
\]
\[
	y - 3z = 0 \Rightarrow y = 0
\]
\[
	x - \frac{1}{2}y - \frac{3}{2}z = 0 \Rightarrow x = 0
\]

Therefore: $x = 0, y = 0, z = 0$
